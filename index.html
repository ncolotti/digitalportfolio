My Projects...

During my internship at Amazon in Seattle this summer, I collaborated with an internal services team to develop secure tooling that improved operational efficiency for various teams across Amazon. One of my key contributions was implementing an end-to-end commenting feature in an AWS security service, which allowed for detailed command execution tracking to ensure contingent authorization. Additionally, I created integration tests for the commenting feature, leading to its successful launch into the beta development pipeline. This experience honed my skills in cloud services, secure software development, and cross-functional teamwork in a high-impact environment.

The Adoption Share project was part of my work on the Data Science for Social Services Team at Cornell, where I worked last semester. Our team of three partnered with Adoption Share Inc. to analyze the AFCARS dataset, focusing on improving child-parent matching and providing recommendations for the company's expansion into new states. I collaborated closely with company contacts and leveraged Tableau to create impactful data visualizations that highlighted key statistics about children in the U.S. foster care system. The insights we generated helped Adoption Share make data-driven decisions to enhance their services and ultimately improve outcomes for children and prospective parents.

Here's what you're seeing: The image shows the poster that my team created for the ORIE research fair at Cornell, which describes our work, findings, and the results we achieved in collaboration with Adoption Share.

My Yahtzee project was a collaborative effort where I worked with a team of four to design, implement, and test a 1,600-line OCaml version of the classic dice game, Yahtzee. We developed a tiered difficulty algorithm to simulate players, making the game more engaging for users. This project challenged us to think critically about game mechanics, AI simulation, and user interaction while working with functional programming in OCaml.

Cornell Concrete Canoe is an exciting team that focuses on designing and constructing canoes made out of sustainably engineered concrete. Since October 2021, I have been involved in researching and creating three unique canoes, contributing to every aspect of the construction process. Our team participates in an annual competition where we not only race the canoe but also present our engineering and design process. I have played a key role in training new members in technical construction skills, ensuring adherence to safety regulations, and fostering a collaborative work environment.
